Stay Safe Online: Prevention Checklist for College Students

8 Actionable Prevention Tips

[cite_start]Never enter a UPI PIN to receive money: Remember that your UPI PIN is only required when you are sending funds or making a payment, never for receiving money.
[cite_start]Do not scan unknown QR codes: Scammers frequently send malicious QR codes via WhatsApp claiming you've won a cash reward; scanning these will authorize a deduction from your account.
[cite_start]Verify internship offers independently: Always cross-check unprompted job or internship emails by visiting the company's official website and contacting their HR department directly before sharing any documents.
[cite_start]Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Turn on 2FA for your college email, banking apps, and social media accounts to ensure a leaked password isn't enough to compromise your identity.
[cite_start]Beware of urgent SMS/WhatsApp links: Ignore unsolicited messages claiming your bank account is blocked, a package is delayed, or your SIM is expiring, as these usually contain phishing links.
[cite_start]Avoid public Wi-Fi for sensitive transactions: Never pay your college fees or access your banking portals while connected to open, public Wi-Fi networks in cafes or on campus.
[cite_start]Limit personal information on social media: Keep your profiles private and never post pictures of your college ID, Aadhar card, driver's license, or travel tickets online.
[cite_start]Regularly monitor your financial statements: Make it a monthly habit to check your bank statements and free credit reports for any unauthorized activities or instant loans taken out in your name.
